ASCENDANT — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 4, 2026

ASCENDANT (package com.mhurston.ascendant) is an offline fitness-tracking app developed by Michael G. Hurston. This policy describes what data the app handles and how. The short version: everything stays on your device; nothing is collected, transmitted, sold, or shared.

Data the app stores

ASCENDANT stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Workout logs you enter (reps, distances, activities, rest days).
  • Body metrics you enter (age, sex, height, weight, goal weight, calorie intake).
  • Journal entries (notes and mood ratings).
  • App settings (units, avatar choice, reminders, video favorites).

The app has no user accounts, no server component, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs that collect data. It never transmits your data off the device.

Health Connect

If you enable the optional "Sync steps & activity" feature, ASCENDANT requests read-only access to two Health Connect data types:

  • Steps
  • Active calories burned

This data is used solely to credit your daily activity inside the app (progress rings and experience points). It is stored in the app's local database on your device, is never written back to Health Connect, and is never transmitted or shared with anyone. You can revoke this access at any time in Health Connect settings or by turning the feature off in the app; the app continues to work fully without it.

Notifications

The optional daily reminder is generated locally on your device. No notification data leaves the device.

Backups and exports

You can export your data (CSV or JSON) at any time. Export files are created only when you choose to create them, saved to the location you pick, and are under your control. The app does not upload them anywhere.

Data deletion

All app data lives on your device. Uninstalling the app, or clearing its storage in Android settings, permanently deletes everything the app stored.

Children

The app is a general-audience fitness tracker and does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone — including children — because it does not collect personal information at all.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app changes how data is handled, this policy will be updated and the effective date revised before that version is released.
